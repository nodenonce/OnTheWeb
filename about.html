Inception of NodeNonce

The idea for NodeNonce all started when the founder, Amiris Brown, stumbled upon a for sale cryptocurrency business listing on Craigslist. After Brown got done hysterically laughing at how much this appeared to be a scam due to the unprofessional look of these offers, Brown began to wonder where people actually list their entire mining operations for sale. So out of curiosity, Brown started to search the web. To Brown's surprise, there was no one offering this listing service that they could easily find. For the most part, people were listing houses outfitted for cryptocurrency mining operations on business–for–sale sites and Craigslist. Brown thought that surely this was not an ideal way to list such property in connecting buyers & sellers and hosting providers with clients. Not to mention, it seemed that the main sites offering rentals for cryptocurrency mining were those of hourly short–term rentals or questionable mining pool operations, of which both rent out only their equipment and do not list any other competitors to be rented. Thus, Brown thought that the cryptocurrency community needed a Real Estate listing site to buy, sell, and rent out their physical cryptocurrency mining operations in a one–stop–shop user friendly way. Whether selling an entire cryptocurrency facility as a fully equipped turn–key business, or just renting out one miner from a rig — NodeNonce is here to list it all! As NodeNonce remains in service to further strengthen the process of consensus, whereby aiding in the decentralization and democratization of the overall crypto space.

Founder of NodeNonce

Amiris Brown, back–in–the–day held numerous pseudo–anonymous handles on various technology and counter–culture online forums. There, during Brown's downtime from their then computer repair business, DuelWave, Brown debated about socio–economic alternatives for a better world. Thus, Brown had known about BitCoin as early as 2008. Yet, Brown took the stance of debating against BitCoin, just as Brown had debated against previous cryptocurrency proposals before that time and all throughout 2010. It was not until the United States election results of 2016 that Brown was finally compelled to ironically enter into the cryptocurrency space. By then, Brown had long lost access to most of their tech equipment when they had walked away from DuelWave during 2009 to pursue their passion in the arts within higher education. Yet in 2016, Brown came to deem various cryptocurrencies as the only contingency plan for current methods of basic bill payments that would defy the threats of increasing fascism set to enact censorship around the world. Earlier that same year of 2016, a flood hit the residential rental that Amiris Brown was cohabitating in with a significant–other at that time. It was then that Brown ended up battling a slum lord. During that legal battle to either get repairs done or break the rental lease, Brown stumbled upon Real Estate property tax sales in lieu of opening up communications with the local Code Enforcement Officers in regards to recording the slum lord's negligence. It was by finding the listed tax sales that Amiris Brown realized getting into Real Estate was not as out of reach as previously thought. However turbulence soon hit in Brown's personal life during 2017, which included dealing with an expensive health issue that was eventually exacerbated by a malpractice done against them. Such preoccupations of self care prevented the full actualization of Brown's Real Estate goals at that time, as effort was spent in fundraising for remedying the potentially deadly infection caused by the malpractice. Eventually, an anonymous BitCoin donor saved Amiris Brown's life by sending enough BitCoin Satoshis to cover the corrective surgery in–full. Despite such grace, the difficulties continued to compound with the unexpected ending of Brown's romantic relationship in 2017. By 2018 Brown was left homeless. Aside from the occasional temporary minimum wage job market, Amiris Brown has long been shut out of the traditional job market since the age of 19 due to their publicly known activism. So it was not exactly easy to regain a footing upon becoming homeless, again. Brown continued to search for ways of gaining an income outside of a job while lacking access to credit, and eventually had an epiphany for a cryptocurrency mining rental & sale listings website/platform. Today, Amiris Brown is currently the sole proprietor of NodeNonce.
